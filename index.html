About Me

I am Maria Metantomwate, a Physics graduate from IPB University with a strong foundation in quantitative thinking, system analysis, and structured problem-solving. My academic background has trained me to approach complex challenges with a logical and analytical mindset, while remaining adaptable in dynamic environments.

Throughout my journey, I have been involved in research, technical projects, and collaborative experiences across different fields. At the National Research and Innovation Agency (BRIN), I worked on micromagnetic simulations to study system behavior, while also developing the ability to translate complex outputs into clear and meaningful insights.

Beyond research, I enjoy building practical solutions through technology. I have developed projects such as an IoT-based environmental monitoring system and other engineering prototypes, combining hardware and software to address real-world problems. I have also participated in national and international robotics competitions, gaining hands-on experience in designing, building, and coordinating multi-disciplinary projects. Additionally, I have foundational knowledge of UAVs and basic proficiency in operating DJI drones, further expanding my technical skill set.

These experiences have shaped my approach to problem-solving: understanding systems holistically, breaking down complexity, and continuously learning new tools and technologies.

As a fresh graduate, I am open to exploring opportunities across technology, engineering, and data analytics. I am particularly interested in roles that allow me to learn, grow, and contribute to impactful solutions.
